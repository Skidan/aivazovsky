Ivan Aivazovsky

the Person

Ivan Aivazovsky, the Armenian-Russian painter, undoubtedly was one of the most fascinating and richly talented artists of the past two centuries. Ethnically an Armenian, he was born the city of Feodosiya which was then a part of the Russian Empire. Today Feodosiya is a port city in the Crimean* Ukraine.

Although Aivazovsky was born in 1817 into a poor family, his father did an amazing job of providing him with a high quality education, teaching him to speak several languages fluently. The young Aivazovsky also showed extraordinary artistic potential from an early age, which proved to be his ticket to a future life of wealth and fame as one of Europe’s most brilliant artists.

Ivan Aivazovsky is by far most known for painting magnificent seascapes, and is considered to be among the best in this category, perhaps “the best” all of history. Fully half of all his life works, which were many, depict ocean scenes with ships, magnificent sunsets, cloudscapes, ships and waves. Other favorite subject matters for Aivazovsky were scenes of the Ukrainian countryside featuring the work and activities of peasants and small rural towns. He also painted rural scenes in the outlying areas of Istanbul.

Aivazovsky was able to travel widely in his long life of 82 years. One of the most significant locations for him would be Istanbul in modern-day Turkey. There he received a number of commissions from the Sultans of the Ottoman Empire. Many of his paintings are still on display today in the magnificent palaces of the former Ottoman rulers.

Short timeline:

1817
Ivan Aivazovsky was born in the city of Feodosia (Theodosia), Crimea, Russian Empire
1833
Aivazovsky arrived in capital of the Russian Empire, Saint Petersburg, to study at the Imperial Academy of Arts
1837
he graduated from the Imperial Academy of Arts with a gold medal, two years earlier than intended, and returned to Feodosia
1840
Aivazovsky was sent by the Imperial Academy of Arts to study in Europe. He visited Venice, Berlin, Vienna, Florence, Amalfi and Sorrento, Naples, Rome
1843
He visited Switzerland, Germany, the Netherlands, Britain, Portugal, Spain and Malta.
1848
Aivazovsky married Julia Graves, an English governess. They had four daughters: Elena (1849), Maria (1851), Alexandra (1852) and Joanne (1858)
1870
Aivazovsky was made an Actual Civil Councilor, the fourth highest civil rank in Russia
1880
Aivazovsky opened an art gallery in his Feodosia house; it became the third museum in the Russian Empire, after the Hermitage Museum and the Tretyakov Gallery.
1882
Aivazovsky married second wife, Anna Burnazian. She was a young Armenian widow 40 years his junior.
1892
After traveling to Paris with his wife, he made a trip to the United States, visiting Niagara Falls in New York and Washington D.C.
1900
Aivazovsky died in Feodosia. In accordance with his wishes, he was buried at the courtyard of St. Sargis Armenian Church.
